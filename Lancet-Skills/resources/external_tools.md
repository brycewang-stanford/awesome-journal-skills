# External Tools & Resources for The Lancet Submissions

A working list of registries, reporting standards, software, and references useful when preparing a clinical or global-health manuscript for *The Lancet*. Always confirm specifics against the current [Lancet Information for Authors](https://www.thelancet.com/for-authors) and the [ICMJE Recommendations](https://www.icmje.org/recommendations/).

## 1. Trial & study registration (prospective, before enrollment)

| Registry | Use / region |
|----------|-------------|
| [ClinicalTrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/) | US and international |
| [ISRCTN](https://www.isrctn.com/) | UK / international |
| [WHO ICTRP](https://trialsearch.who.int/) | global portal aggregating primary registries |
| Regional primary registries | [EU-CTR / CTIS](https://euclinicaltrials.eu/), [ANZCTR](https://www.anzctr.org.au/), [CTRI](https://ctri.nic.in/) (India), [ChiCTR](https://www.chictr.org.cn/) (China), [PACTR](https://pactr.samrc.ac.za/) (Africa) |
| Systematic review registration | [PROSPERO](https://www.crd.york.ac.uk/prospero/) |
| Pre-registration of analyses | [OSF Registrations](https://osf.io/registries) |

> Trials must be **registered prospectively** (before the first participant is enrolled) and the registration number reported in the abstract.

## 2. Reporting guidelines (EQUATOR)

- [EQUATOR Network](https://www.equator-network.org/) — index of reporting guidelines by study type.
- [CONSORT](https://www.consort-statement.org/) — randomised trials (+ extensions: cluster, non-inferiority, pragmatic, harms, PRO) — includes the **flow diagram**.
- [STROBE](https://www.strobe-statement.org/) — observational studies (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional).
- [PRISMA](https://www.prisma-statement.org/) — systematic reviews & meta-analyses (+ PRISMA-P, PRISMA-Equity) — includes the **flow diagram**.
- [SPIRIT](https://www.spirit-statement.org/) — trial protocols.
- [STARD](https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/stard/) — diagnostic accuracy; [TRIPOD](https://www.tripod-statement.org/) — prediction models; [SQUIRE](http://www.squire-statement.org/) — quality improvement; [CHEERS](https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/cheers/) — economic evaluation.

## 3. Ethics, integrity & disclosure

- [ICMJE Recommendations](https://www.icmje.org/recommendations/) — authorship, the disclosure (Declaration of interests) form, data sharing, registration.
- [Declaration of Helsinki (WMA)](https://www.wma.net/policies-post/wma-declaration-of-helsinki/) — ethics of research with human participants.
- [CRediT](https://credit.niso.org/) — contributor-role taxonomy; [ORCID](https://orcid.org/) — author identifiers.
- [COPE](https://publicationethics.org/) — publication-ethics guidance.

## 4. Equity, sex/gender & global-health reporting

- [SAGER guidelines](https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/sager-guidelines/) — Sex And Gender Equity in Research reporting.
- [PROGRESS-Plus](https://methods.cochrane.org/equity/projects/evidence-equity/progress-plus) — equity-relevant participant characteristics.
- [WHO health-equity resources](https://www.who.int/health-topics/health-equity) and [Global Index Medicus](https://www.globalindexmedicus.net/) / [LILACS](https://lilacs.bvsalud.org/) — regional literature for global-health systematic searches.

## 5. Evidence synthesis & certainty

- [GRADE](https://www.gradeworkinggroup.org/) / [GRADEpro GDT](https://www.gradepro.org/) — rating certainty of evidence and strength of recommendations.
- [Cochrane Library](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/) and [Cochrane Handbook](https://training.cochrane.org/handbook) — for the systematic search behind the Research in context panel.
- Search platforms: [PubMed/MEDLINE](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/), [Embase](https://www.embase.com/), [Covidence](https://www.covidence.org/) / [Rayyan](https://www.rayyan.ai/) for screening.

## 6. Statistics & figures

- R ([survival](https://cran.r-project.org/package=survival), [survminer](https://cran.r-project.org/package=survminer) for Kaplan–Meier with numbers at risk, [meta](https://cran.r-project.org/package=meta)/[metafor](https://www.metafor-project.org/) for forest plots, [tableone](https://cran.r-project.org/package=tableone) for Table 1), [Stata](https://www.stata.com/), [SAS](https://www.sas.com/).
- Sample size / power: [PASS](https://www.ncss.com/software/pass/), [G*Power](https://www.psychologie.hhu.de/arbeitsgruppen/allgemeine-psychologie-und-arbeitspsychologie/gpower).
- Reproducibility: record software versions, seeds, and the SAP; archive analysis code where data-sharing permits.
- Colourblind-safe palettes: [viridis](https://bids.github.io/colormap/), [ColorBrewer](https://colorbrewer2.org/), [Okabe-Ito](https://jfly.uni-koeln.de/color/).

## 7. References & writing

- Reference managers: [Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/), [EndNote](https://endnote.com/) — use the Lancet (Vancouver-style numbered) CSL/style; verify on a final pass.
- Journal abbreviations: [NLM Catalog](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nlmcatalog/journals).

## 9. Notes

1. **Prospective registration** is mandatory for clinical trials; retrospective registration may make a trial ineligible — disclose timing honestly.
2. The Lancet requires a **structured abstract** (Background, Methods, Findings, Interpretation, Funding) ≤ ~300 words, a **Research in context** panel for original Articles, a **Declaration of interests**, a **role of the funding source** statement, and a **data sharing statement** — confirm current requirements before submission.
3. Family fit: if the work is rigorous but not practice-changing for a broad global audience, consider *Lancet Global Health*, *Lancet Public Health*, a Lancet specialty title, or *EClinicalMedicine* (open access).
4. Keep the protocol, SAP, and de-identified data ready; editors, statistical reviewers, and the data-sharing policy can require them.
