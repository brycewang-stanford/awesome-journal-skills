# REStud Official Source Map

Primary sources consulted to make this skill pack specific to **The Review of Economic Studies (REStud)** — published by Oxford University Press (OUP) for the Review of Economic Studies Ltd. Each entry lists what it verifies. **Access date for all entries: 2026-05-30.** Numbers, fees, editors, and policies change — always re-verify on the official pages before relying on a specific figure.

## Journal identity, aims & scope, history

- **The Review of Economic Studies — journal homepage (OUP)** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud>
  Verifies: REStud is published by Oxford University Press; top-5 standing; covers theoretical and applied economics.
- **About (OUP)** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/pages/About>
  Verifies: founded **1933** by a group of young British and American economists; object "to encourage research in theoretical and applied economics, especially by young economists"; recognised as one of the core top-five economics journals; the May Meetings tradition since 1989.
- **The Review of Economic Studies — Wikipedia** — <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Review_of_Economic_Studies>
  Verifies (secondary, cross-checked against OUP "About"): founding 1933; original editors Abba Lerner, Paul Sweezy, Ursula Hicks; OUP publishes for the Review of Economic Studies Ltd (UK); landmark papers (Arrow 1962, Mirrlees 1971, Stiglitz 1974).
- **Society / journal site** — <https://www.restud.com>
  Verifies: the Review of Economic Studies Ltd runs the journal; hosts submissions, data policy, tours, and editorial board pages.

## REStud Tour / May Meetings (young-economist tradition)

- **May Meetings (OUP)** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/pages/may_meetings>
  Verifies: the journal's flagship event showcasing promising graduating doctoral students; held annually since 1989.
- **Tours (restud.com)** — <https://www.restud.com/tours/>
  Verifies: the **REStud Tour** (formerly the **May Meetings**) has run annually in **May since 1989**; selected top graduating PhD students present over two days at three or four host institutions; since 2021 a North American tour for European students; since 2026 split into two distinct programs.

## Submission mechanics, fee, anonymity, references, length

- **Submissions (restud.com)** — <https://www.restud.com/submissions/>
  Verifies: **USD 200** new-submission fee; **reduced USD 120** when every author is (i) a current student, (ii) within six years of the PhD, or (iii) resident in a World Bank low/middle-income economy (effective 1 July 2023); supporting evidence required for the reduced rate; **~45-page limit** including title page, tables, figures, references, appendices; 1.5-spaced 12-pt text, ≥1-inch margins; editors pre-screen before refereeing.
- **Instructions to Authors (OUP)** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/pages/general_instructions>
  Verifies: submission portal **Editorial Express** (<http://editorialexpress.com/restud>); **JEL classification required**; data access policy exists; self-archiving embargo (24 months standard / 12 months for some funders).
- **E-submit / Editorial Express portal** — <http://editorialexpress.com/restud> (also <https://editorialexpress.com/cgi-bin/e-editor/e-submit.cgi?dbase=restud>)
  The live submission system.
- **Reference / review style** — confirmed via the OUP instructions and the journal's published articles: **Harvard author-date** referencing; **double-anonymous** refereeing (author and referee identities both hidden). (Both are durable conventions; re-verify the live style guide.)

## Data & code availability / replication

- **Data and Code Availability Policy (restud.com)** — <https://www.restud.com/data-and-code-availability-policy/>
  Verifies: data, programs, and computational details for accepted empirical / numerical / experimental papers; pre-publication reproducibility verification; exemptions for proprietary data (notify editors at submission); materials may be requested at the review stage.
- **The Review of Economic Studies Data Editor** — <https://restud.github.io/data-editor/> (also <https://restud.dataeditor.group/>)
  Verifies: REStud has a **Data Editor (Miklós Koren)**, in place since 2019; follows the **AEA Data and Code Availability Standard (DCAS)**; coordinates reproducibility requirements with other major economics journals.
- **Data and Code Availability (OUP)** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/pages/data-and-code-availability>
  Verifies: OUP-hosted statement of the policy; materials posted alongside articles.
- **Econometric Society — replication-package deposit at Zenodo** — <https://www.econometricsociety.org/publications/es-data-editor-website/package>
  Verifies (corroborating, shared infrastructure): conditionally accepted ES/REStud-family papers deposit replication packages at the **Zenodo** journals community after reproducibility checks; package receives a DOI.
- **AEA DCAS / Zenodo community** — <https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/data/faq> and <https://zenodo.org/communities/aeajournals/about>
  Verifies (secondary): the DCAS standard and Zenodo deposit workflow REStud's policy aligns with.

## Selectivity (secondary)

- **Card & DellaVigna, "What do Editors Maximize?"** — <https://eml.berkeley.edu/~sdellavi/wp/CardDellaVignaEditorsDec17.pdf>
  Verifies (secondary, academic study): REStud desk-rejects roughly **20–23%** of initial submissions — markedly lower than QJE (~61%) and REStat (~56%).

## Representative / landmark REStud papers (exemplars used in skills)

- **Arrow, "The Economic Implications of Learning by Doing," REStud 29(3), 1962, 155–173** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/29/3/155/1530360> (JSTOR <https://www.jstor.org/stable/2295952>)
  Foundational endogenous-growth paper.
- **Cass, "Optimum Growth in an Aggregative Model of Capital Accumulation," REStud 32(3), 1965, 233–240** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/32/3/233/1551001> (JSTOR <https://www.jstor.org/stable/2295827>)
  Foundation of the Ramsey–Cass–Koopmans model.
- **Mirrlees, "An Exploration in the Theory of Optimum Income Taxation," REStud 38(2), 1971, 175–208** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/38/2/175/1527903>
  Nobel-cited; the canonical REStud optimal-taxation theory paper.
- **Stiglitz, "Incentives and Risk Sharing in Sharecropping," REStud 41(2), 1974, 219–255** — <https://academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/41/2/219/1531732> (JSTOR <https://www.jstor.org/stable/2296714>)
  Landmark contract-theory / information-economics paper.
