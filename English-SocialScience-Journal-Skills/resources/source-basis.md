# Source Basis and Official-Check Notes

This pack encodes journal-fit and house-style heuristics for the 100 mainstream
English-language economics / finance / management / accounting / marketing /
operations / information-systems journals on the parent repository's roadmap. It
is a positioning and re-framing aid, **not** a static substitute for any journal's
live author instructions.

For per-journal starting queries, use `official-source-map.md` in this directory.

## Roadmap and ranking lists consulted while building this expansion

- Root repository roadmap: `README.md`, section "English — 100 mainstream econ & management journals" (Economics 50 · Finance 13 · Management/Strategy/Org 15 · Marketing 6 · Accounting 6 · Operations & Information Systems 10).
- Economics tiers: the "top-5" convention (AER, QJE, JPE, Econometrica, REStud) and RePEc/IDEAS aggregate journal rankings.

- Society / publisher affiliations that are stable and public (AEA, Econometric Society, AFA, SFS, EFA, AOM, SMS, AIB, INFORMS, AIS, AAA, AMA, POMS, and the relevant university presses / Wiley / Elsevier / OUP / SAGE imprints).

## What each profile deliberately does NOT assert

To stay correct over time, every profile **omits volatile facts** and defers them
to the official-submission checklist instead of stating a number:

- No impact factors, CiteScores, acceptance rates, or "ranked #N" claims.
- No ISSNs, exact word/figure limits, submission fees, or editor names.
- No fabricated citations (literatures are referred to generically).

Stable, widely documented facts (discipline, top-5 / top-3 membership, society
publisher, permanent submission artifacts such as the AEA data-and-code policy,
RFS registered-reports track, Cell-style STAR Methods analogues, or the
department-editor model at Management Science) are used where they help fit.

## Operational rule for every journal skill

1. Before submitting, search the **live official site or submission system** for
   "<journal name> submission guidelines / information for authors" and follow the
   current version.
2. Trust the publisher's own domain, the learned-society page, or the journal's
   editorial-manager / ScholarOne / Editorial Express system over any third-party
   or aggregator copy.
3. Re-check anonymization / double-blind rules, word and figure limits,
   abstract / JEL or keyword codes, reference style, data & code availability and
   replication policy, pre-registration / registered-report options, ethics and
   conflict-of-interest disclosure, AI-use disclosure, and submission fees.
4. If the journal belongs to an umbrella publisher or society, check both the
   journal-specific page and the umbrella policy page; submission portals often
   add requirements not visible on the journal landing page.
5. If the live official instructions conflict with a skill, the official
   instructions win.
