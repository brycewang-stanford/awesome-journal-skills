# JACS Manuscript Template

A structural skeleton for a JACS Article or Communication plus the Supporting
Information. Adapt section depth to the format; verify current formatting and
limits on the JACS author page. Use the ACS Word/LaTeX template for the real
submission.

## Main Text

### Introduction

- Frame the chemical problem and the gap for a broad chemistry readership.
- State the closest prior art and its limitation.
- End with a clear thesis: what this paper shows.

### Results and Discussion

#### Synthesis / reaction development
- Key result and conditions (optimization details → SI).
- Scheme 1: central transformation with conditions and yield.

#### Scope and selectivity
- Representative scope figure (yields, ee/dr); note failed substrates.

#### Structural characterization
- Decisive characterization (NMR/HRMS/IR; X-ray figure + CCDC number).

#### Mechanism (if applicable)
- Control experiments, kinetics, KIE, labeling, intermediates.
- Proposed cycle (mass- and charge-balanced); distinguish shown vs proposed.

### Conclusion

- What is now established and what is newly enabled across chemistry.

### Associated Content

- Supporting Information statement; data availability statement.

### Author Information

- Corresponding author, ORCIDs, author contributions, notes (conflicts).

### Acknowledgments

- Funding and facilities (kept out of double-blind text if applicable).

### References

- ACS numbered style, in citation order, abbreviated journal titles.

## Supporting Information (separate PDF)

### General experimental
- Materials/sources; solvent/reagent purification; inert-atmosphere conditions.
- Instruments and conditions (NMR field/solvent, HRMS mode, IR mode, diffractometer).
- Safety/hazard notes.

### General procedures
- Representative procedures (GP-1, GP-2, ...) with scale, equivalents, T, time, workup, purification.

### Compound characterization
For each new compound (by bold number):
> **Compound name (n).** Procedure. Yield (mg, %). Appearance.
> ¹H NMR (field, solvent): δ ... (mult, J, nH). ¹³C{¹H} NMR (field, solvent): δ ...
> [¹⁹F/³¹P/etc. as relevant]. IR (mode): ν ... cm⁻¹. HRMS (mode) m/z calcd for
> [formula] [adduct], found. [α]D / mp / UV-vis as appropriate.
> Purity: EA calcd/found, or HPLC/GC trace. ee/dr with method (chiral column...).

### Copies of spectra
- ¹H, ¹³C (and diagnostic 2D/heteronuclear) for every new compound, labeled.
- Chiral HPLC/GC/SFC traces (racemate + sample).

### Crystallographic data
- Crystal data and refinement table per structure.
- CCDC deposition number(s); checkCIF/PLATON notes.

### Mechanistic / kinetic data
- Control-experiment results; kinetic plots and rate laws; KIE; labeling.

### Computational details (if used)
- Method, basis set/pseudopotentials, solvation, software + version.
- Optimized coordinates (xyz) and energies; stationary-point characterization.

## Notes on format

- Compound numbers in **bold**; the same number always means the same structure.
- SI units, italic variables (J, k, T), spaces between number and unit.
- Figure/scheme count disciplined; full data in SI, not the main text.
- Communication: keep the main text concise; full proof lives in the SI.
