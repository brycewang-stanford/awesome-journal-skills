# Source Basis and Official-Check Notes

This pack encodes journal-fit and house-style heuristics for 100 mainstream
English-language **natural-science, clinical/medical, physical, and formal-science**
journals — the "other 100" complementing the sibling econ/finance/management bundle
`English-SocialScience-Journal-Skills`. It is a positioning and re-framing aid,
**not** a static substitute for any journal's live author instructions.

## What each profile deliberately does NOT assert

To stay correct over time, every profile **omits volatile facts** and defers them
to the official-submission checklist instead of stating a number:

- No impact factors, CiteScores, acceptance rates, or "ranked #N" claims.
- No ISSNs, exact word/figure limits, APC amounts, or editor names.
- No fabricated citations (literatures are referred to generically).

Stable, widely documented facts (discipline; society/publisher; permanent
structural features such as the PRL length limit, Cell STAR Methods, the Nature
reporting summary / extended data system, the eLife Reviewed-Preprint model, PNAS
Direct Submission, the largely-invited nature of Chemical Reviews / Reviews of
Modern Physics / Accounts of Chemical Research / CA / Trends in Cognitive Sciences,
and CONSORT/trial-registration expectations at major medical journals) are used
where they help fit.

## Operational rule for every journal skill

1. Before submitting, search the **live official site or submission system** for
   "<journal name> author instructions / information for authors" and follow the
   current version.
2. Trust the publisher's own domain, the learned-society page, or the journal's
   submission system (Editorial Manager / ScholarOne / eJournalPress / the Nature
   and Cell Press systems) over any third-party or aggregator copy.
3. Re-check article-type and length limits, abstract format (structured vs.
   unstructured), figure/display limits, the applicable reporting guideline and
   checklist (CONSORT, PRISMA, STROBE, ARRIVE, MDAR), data / code / materials
   availability and deposition, registration / ethics / consent, competing-interests
   and funding disclosure, AI-use disclosure, preprint policy, and open-access /
   licensing terms.
4. If the live official instructions conflict with a skill, the official
   instructions win.
